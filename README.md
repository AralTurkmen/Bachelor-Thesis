As the demand for electricity continues to increase, power generation must scale accordingly while minimizing additional environmental impact. One approach to achieving this is through the expanded implementation of solar and wind power generation. However, due to the intermittent nature of these energy sources, controllable backup systems are required during periods of low production. This creates a need for a balance between power grid expansion and hydropower simulation to ensure stable and reliable grid operation. This study therefore aims to provide a comprehensive guide for modeling a complex hydropower system. The open-source program Spine Toolbox together with SpineOpt were used to model the hydropower system of the Ångermanälven, as they provide the foundation of an already capable energy system simulation framework. In addition, a structured Excel based setup was developed to simplify the adjustment and import of model parameters. The resulting model successfully interconnects and simulates all power plants with realistic water flows, including effects such as spillage, environmental inflow, and real world electricity prices. Although the model does not yet achieve full real-world accuracy, it provides a solid foundation for further development and represents a useful step toward supporting future environmental and energy goals.
